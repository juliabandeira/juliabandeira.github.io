Who I am

I’m the youngest child of four siblings. Born in the least fun region of Brazil (the south), my childhood/teenage years would make Eleanor Shellstrop scream “ya basic”.

I’m a political scientist. As I grew up I started to wonder how the world works, and why it works the way it does. Therefore, when I had to choose the path I would trail, political science was an obvious choice. During my undergrad and graduate degrees, I leaned over the different political systems, studying from how unicameralism impacts decision-making to what guides the vote of a citizen in the smallest rural areas. Throughout this period I also learned that politics could be intensively dreadful.

I’m a Ph.D. student dropout. Although I had a decade-long research career, publishing book chapters and articles in leading academic journals (you can check my academic CV here), I decided that I want to work with something less chaotic than Latin American politics.

As a political scientist, I was trained to think in a completely different way than a front-end developer thinks since politics frequently are not based on logic. And that’s exactly what draws me into the front-end developer career: work with something immerse in logic to try to solve problems that actually can be solved. Also, I think that the tech industry, on one hand, is a potent instrument to increase inequalities (between individuals and between countries) and foster totalitarian aims, while on the other hand, it may contribute to the exact opposite. In other words, the tech industry can promote, more than any other industry, equality and democratization. In this sense, I hope that someday my work contributes to this aspect.

I’m a front-end developer. Or, maybe I’m not, since I have zero professional experience, but I’m eager to have a chance to start this new path. I love to learn and I grasp new knowledge quickly, so I’m sure I will be a valuable asset wherever I land.
